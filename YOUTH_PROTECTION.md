# Parents for Scouting, Inc. (PFSI) — Youth Protection & Digital Safety Standard
**Document Owner:** Chief Executive Officer (CEO)  
**Document Approver:** Chief Technology Officer (CTO)    
**Applies To:** All PFSI technology systems, online platforms, software projects, and collaboration tools (including GitHub)  
**Effective Date:** 2026-01-25  
**Version:** 1.0

---

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this standard is to ensure that all technology use within Parents for Scouting, Inc. (PFSI) protects youth, follows the principles of Scouting America Youth Protection and digital safety guidance, and provides safe, transparent, and well-supervised online environments.

Youth protection takes priority over convenience, speed, or technical preferences.

---

## 2. Governing Principles
PFSI adopts the following principles for all digital systems and online activities:

1. **Youth safety comes first.**
2. **No one-on-one, unobservable interactions between adults and youth.**
3. **Transparency and auditability in all online collaboration.**
4. **Two-deep leadership applies in digital spaces.**
5. **Least privilege and age-appropriate access.**
6. **No storage or transmission of youth personal data in developer or collaboration platforms like GitHub.**

---

## 3. Alignment with Scouting America
PFSI’s technology practices are designed to align with:
- The Guide to Safe Scouting (GSS)
- Scouting America digital safety guidance for online activities
- Youth Protection policies and reporting requirements

Where there is any conflict or uncertainty, **Scouting America policy takes precedence**.

---

## 4. Scope
This policy applies to:
- All PFSI leaders, volunteers, contractors, and vendors
- Any youth who participate in PFSI technology or STEM activities
- All PFSI-managed systems including (but not limited to):
  - GitHub
  - Websites
  - Email systems
  - Collaboration tools
  - Code repositories
  - Learning platforms
  - Cloud services

---

## 5. Absolute Rules (Non-Negotiable)

1. **No private 1:1 adult–youth communication channels** in any PFSI-managed system.
   - This includes direct messages, private chats, private issues, or off-platform side channels for PFSI activities.

2. **All youth-involved collaboration must occur in observable, logged spaces** with adult oversight.
   - Examples: GitHub Issues and Pull Requests, shared project boards visible to maintainers.

3. **No youth personal data in GitHub or developer tools.**
   - Rosters, names + contact info, advancement data, medical info, incident reports, or screenshots containing such data are strictly prohibited.

4. **At least two registered adults must have access and oversight** for any system, repository, or project involving youth.

5. **Youth never have administrative control** over systems, repositories, or accounts.

6. **No sensitive discussions about youth** (discipline, incidents, allegations, health, behavior) in technology platforms.

---

## 6. Approved Participation Model for Youth

Youth participation in technology projects is allowed only when:

- The project/repository is explicitly approved by the CTO or designee as **Youth-Eligible**
- The repository/system:
  - Is access-controlled
  - Uses Pull Requests and review workflows
  - Has at least **two adult maintainers**
  - Has required review before changes are merged

### Youth Access Restrictions
Youth participants:
- May contribute via branches and Pull Requests
- May comment in Issues and PRs
- May NOT:
  - Be admins, owners, or maintainers
  - Approve their own changes
  - Manage users or settings
  - Use private messaging for project work

---

## 7. Communication Standards

All PFSI technology-related communication involving youth must be:

- Professional
- On-topic
- Logged and reviewable
- Visible to multiple adults

Prohibited:
- Private direct messages between one adult and one youth
- Disappearing messages
- Side-channel coordination (text, personal Discord, etc.) for PFSI projects

If off-platform communication is required (e.g., scheduling), it must follow Scouting America’s youth communication rules and include appropriate adult oversight.

---

## 8. Content Standards

Prohibited in any PFSI technology platform:
- Sexual, abusive, or inappropriate content
- Harassment, bullying, or intimidation
- Political advocacy
- Any content that violates Scouting America policies or PFSI Code of Conduct

All collaboration spaces must remain suitable for youth participation at all times.

---

## 9. Privacy & Data Protection

- Youth personal data must only be stored in **approved systems designed for that purpose** (e.g., official Scouting platforms).
- Developer tools, collaboration platforms, and code repositories are **not** approved systems of record for youth information.
- Photos or media identifying youth may only be used if already approved for public use and appropriate permissions exist.

---

## 10. Incident Reporting & Escalation

Any of the following must be **reported immediately** to PFSI leadership:

- Suspected grooming behavior
- Boundary violations
- Attempts to move communication to private channels
- Exposure or sharing of youth personal data
- Inappropriate content or interactions

For any situation involving potential abuse or policy violation, follow **Scouting America reporting requirements** and PFSI escalation procedures.

Do **not** investigate on your own. Do **not** attempt to quietly “handle it.”

---

## 11. Enforcement

- The CTO and PFSI leadership may immediately:
  - Remove access
  - Lock accounts or repositories
  - Suspend systems or projects
to protect youth and the organization.

- Violations may result in:
  - Permanent removal from PFSI technology systems
  - Additional organizational or legal actions as required

---

## 12. Training & Awareness

- All adults involved in youth-facing technology activities must:
  - Be current in Scouting America Youth Protection Training (YPT)
  - Review this policy annually
- Youth will be given age-appropriate guidance on safe and appropriate online conduct.
