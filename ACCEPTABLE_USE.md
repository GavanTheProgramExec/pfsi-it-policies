# Parents for Scouting, Inc. (PFSI) — Acceptable Use Policy
**Document Owner:** Chief Technology Officer (CTO)  
**Applies To:** All users of PFSI technology systems, services, accounts, and platforms (including GitHub)  
**Effective Date:** 2026-01-25  
**Version:** 1.0

---

## 1. Purpose
This Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) defines what is and is not permitted when using PFSI technology systems. Its goals are to:
- Protect youth
- Protect privacy and security
- Maintain professional, Scout-like conduct
- Protect PFSI’s reputation and operations
- Ensure systems are used only for appropriate organizational purposes

---

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
- All PFSI leaders, volunteers, members, contractors, and vendors
- Any youth who are granted access to PFSI technology systems
- All PFSI-managed or PFSI-provided systems including:
  - GitHub
  - Websites
  - Email systems
  - Collaboration tools
  - Cloud services
  - Software and data systems

---

## 3. Core Principles
1. **Youth protection comes first.**
2. **Use PFSI systems only for PFSI-approved purposes.**
3. **Be professional, respectful, and Scout-like in all communications.**
4. **Assume your actions are logged and reviewable.**
5. **When in doubt, do not post or share.**

---

## 4. Acceptable Use (What IS Allowed)

Users may:
- Use PFSI systems to support approved PFSI programs and operations
- Collaborate on code, documents, and projects in approved repositories and platforms
- Communicate in Issues, Pull Requests, and other approved, observable collaboration spaces
- Use test or sample data that contains **no real personal information**
- Report security or policy concerns without fear of retaliation

---

## 5. Prohibited Use (What is NOT Allowed)

Users must NOT:

### 5.1 Youth Safety & Conduct
- Engage in **private one-on-one communication** with a youth in any PFSI-managed system
- Attempt to move PFSI-related youth communication to personal or off-platform channels
- Share, request, or store youth personal data in non-approved systems (including GitHub)
- Discuss youth discipline, behavior, medical issues, or incidents in technology platforms

### 5.2 Data & Security
- Store or share passwords, API keys, tokens, or other credentials in code, documents, or messages
- Attempt to bypass security controls, access restrictions, or audit mechanisms
- Upload malware, backdoors, crypto-miners, or intentionally vulnerable code
- Introduce tools or services without Technology Department approval

### 5.3 Content & Behavior
- Post or transmit:
  - Harassing, abusive, discriminatory, or threatening content
  - Sexual or inappropriate material
  - Political advocacy or campaigning
  - Copyright-infringing material
- Use PFSI systems for personal business, commercial ventures, or non-PFSI causes
- Impersonate others or misrepresent authority or identity

### 5.4 Systems & Resources
- Use PFSI systems for excessive personal use or any illegal activity
- Attempt to access systems, data, or accounts without authorization
- Modify or delete logs, audit trails, or security records

---

## 6. Account Responsibility
Users are responsible for:
- All activity performed with their account
- Keeping their credentials secure
- Using strong passwords and enabling required two-factor authentication (2FA)
- Logging out of shared or public computers

Accounts must not be shared.

---

## 7. Privacy Expectations
- Users should have **no expectation of privacy** when using PFSI systems.
- Activity may be logged, monitored, and reviewed to protect youth, security, and the organization.
- Monitoring is for safety and governance, not routine surveillance.

---

## 8. Violations & Enforcement
Violations of this policy may result in:
- Immediate removal of system access
- Removal from technology projects or roles
- Escalation to PFSI leadership
- Additional actions as required by PFSI policy or Scouting America rules

For serious matters (especially youth safety), access may be revoked **immediately** without prior notice.

---

## 9. Reporting Concerns
All users are encouraged and expected to report:
- Security issues
- Policy violations
- Youth safety concerns
- Inappropriate behavior

Reports should be made to the CTO or designated PFSI leadership.  
Do not attempt to investigate serious matters on your own.

---

## 10. Acknowledgement
Access to PFSI systems constitutes agreement to comply with:
- This Acceptable Use Policy
- The PFSI Youth Protection & Digital Safety Standard
- The PFSI Data Classification Standard
- All applicable PFSI and Scouting America policies
